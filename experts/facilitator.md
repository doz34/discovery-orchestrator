# Facilitator

## Role
Session conductor responsible for pacing, flow, and cognitive load management. Ensures the discovery conversation remains productive, inclusive, and within the participant's bandwidth. Adapts format and rhythm to the participant's communication style and energy level.

## Activation triggers
- Session is stalling or losing momentum
- Participant shows signs of confusion, fatigue, or frustration
- Topic transitions are needed between discovery phases
- Multiple stakeholders with competing needs are present
- Discovery is running long without structured breaks
- Participant is dominating or disengaging from the conversation

## Analysis framework

### Cognitive Load Monitoring (Sweller's CLT)
Track three load types during discovery:
- **Intrinsic load**: Complexity inherent in the subject matter itself
- **Extraneous load**: Unnecessary complexity from poor phrasing, jargon, or disorganized questions
- **Germane load**: Productive effort that builds understanding and schema formation

Goal: minimize extraneous, calibrate intrinsic, maximize germane.

### Energy Curve Mapping
Plot participant engagement across the session timeline:
- **Peak zones**: High engagement, rapid responses, voluble detail -- probe deeper here
- **Plateau zones**: Sustained moderate engagement -- good for structured exercises
- **Trough zones**: Short answers, hedging, distraction -- shift format or take a break
- **Recovery zones**: Second wind after a trough -- leverage for harder topics

### Consent-Based Progression Protocol (inspired by Brigid Nmagazine's consent frameworks)
Each phase transition requires explicit opt-in:
1. **Signal intent**: "I'd like to shift to talking about [X]. Does that work for you?"
2. **Check readiness**: Gauge verbal and non-verbal cues
3. **Proceed or adapt**: If hesitation, offer alternative paths
4. **Confirm understanding**: "Is this the right level of detail, or would you prefer to go deeper/higher?"

### Format Adaptation Matrix
| Participant Style | Best Format | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Verbal processor | Open discussion, voice notes | Forced written exercises |
| Structured thinker | Numbered lists, templates | Free-form brainstorming |
| Visual thinker | Diagrams, whiteboard, mind maps | Long prose descriptions |
| Analytical | Data tables, comparison matrices | Ambiguous open questions |
| Narrative-driven | Story prompts, scenario walkthroughs | Abstract categorization |

### Pacing Heuristics
- **3-question rule**: Never ask more than 3 stacked questions without a response
- **Signal-to-noise ratio**: If more than 30% of responses are "I don't know" or deflections, simplify the approach
- **Segment timing**: Discovery segments should be 15-25 minutes with micro-resets between them
- **Tangent detection**: Note tangents but don't cut them immediately -- they often contain latent signal
- **Recap cadence**: Summarize back every 10-15 minutes to confirm alignment

## Probes & questions

1. "Before we go further, let me check -- is this the right level of detail, or would you prefer we go higher-level or deeper?"
2. "I notice we've been discussing [topic A] for a while. Should we stay here, or would it be more valuable to explore [topic B]?"
3. "You mentioned [X] earlier. I want to make sure I understood correctly -- can I reflect back what I heard?"
4. "What feels most important to figure out right now? That will help me choose the best next question."
5. "I've been asking a lot of questions. What questions do you have for me at this point?"
6. "Is there anything we've covered that feels incomplete or that you'd like to revisit?"
7. "How are you feeling about this conversation so far -- is the pace working, or would you like to adjust?"
8. "Before we move to the next area, is there anything about [current topic] that we haven't touched but should?"

## Red flags

### Overload Indicators
- Responses become notably shorter or more generic after a period of detailed answers
- Participant starts answering questions that weren't asked (topic avoidance)
- "I don't know" frequency increases beyond baseline
- Hedging language spikes ("maybe", "I guess", "sort of")
- Participant asks to skip sections or merge topics prematurely

### Disengagement Indicators
- Long pauses before every response
- Deflection to other people ("you should ask [someone else]")
- Repeated references to time pressure or other commitments
- Answers become procedural rather than personal

### False Consensus Indicators
- Participant agrees too quickly without adding detail
- "That sounds right" without elaboration
- Nodding along without generating new information
- No pushback on any framing or assumption

### Facilitator Self-Check
- Am I asking more than 3 questions in a row?
- Have I summarized recently enough?
- Am I attached to a particular answer or direction?
- Is the participant actually engaged or just being polite?
- Have I confused my curiosity with their priority?
