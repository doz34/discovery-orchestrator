# Phase 2: Friction & Cost

## Goal
Quantify the pain: what is the current workaround costing in time, money, emotion, and opportunity?

## Entry condition
At least one concrete case is documented from Phase 1 with timeline, actors, and outcomes.

## Actions

1. **Map current workarounds.** For each friction point identified in the concrete case, determine what the user does today to cope. Be specific: "You said you copy-paste into a spreadsheet — walk me through exactly how that works."

2. **Calculate time cost.** Estimate hours per occurrence, frequency per week/month, and annualized total. Ask the user to validate: "So that's about 3 hours per incident, twice a month — roughly 72 hours a year. Does that sound right?" Include setup time, cleanup time, and context-switching overhead.

3. **Calculate money cost.** Convert time to dollars using the user's or their team's loaded cost rate. Include direct costs (tools, services, errors that cost money) and indirect costs (opportunity, delays). Ask: "If you could get those 72 hours back, what would you use them for?"

4. **Assess emotional cost.** Use the emotional markers from Phase 1. Categorize:
   - **Frustration**: tasks that irritate but get done
   - **Avoidance**: tasks that get skipped or delegated because they are painful
   - **Anxiety**: tasks that cause worry about consequences if done wrong
   - **Resignation**: tasks accepted as "just how it is" — these often hide the deepest pain

5. **Identify opportunity cost.** What is not happening because of this friction? What projects, features, clients, or improvements are on hold? Ask: "If this problem disappeared tomorrow, what's the first thing you would do with the freed capacity?"

6. **Compute the status quo cost.** Synthesize all costs into a single summary: "Doing nothing costs you approximately [X hours/year], [Y dollars/year], and prevents [Z opportunity]." Make this tangible and specific to their situation.

7. **Stress-test the cost estimate.** Ask the user to push back: "Am I overcounting? Undercounting? What did I miss?" Adjust based on their corrections.

## Suggested prompts

- "What's your current workaround for this? Walk me through exactly what you do today."
- "How often does this happen? How long does each instance take, start to finish?"
- "What would you do with those hours if you got them back?"
- "Are there things you simply don't do because this is too painful? What are you avoiding?"
- "If nothing changes, what does this look like in a year? Worse, same, better?"

## Exit condition
Current workarounds are mapped, costs are quantified across four dimensions (time, money, emotion, opportunity), and a status quo cost statement is validated by the user.

## Output
- Workaround inventory with step-by-step descriptions
- Time cost calculation (per instance, frequency, annualized)
- Money cost calculation (direct + indirect)
- Emotional cost map (frustration/avoidance/anxiety/resignation)
- Opportunity cost inventory
- Status quo cost summary statement (validated by user)
