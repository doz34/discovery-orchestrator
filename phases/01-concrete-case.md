# Phase 1: Concrete Case

## Goal
Anchor the discovery in a specific, real, recent example instead of abstract generalizations.

## Entry condition
Phase 0 orientation is complete. Domain, complexity track, and initial framing are established.

## Actions

1. **Ask for a specific recent occurrence.** Do not accept "usually" or "sometimes" or "in general." Push for the last time this happened. Get a date or timeframe. Example: "Tell me about the most recent time this caused a problem — when exactly was that?"

2. **Reconstruct the timeline of that specific event.** Walk through it chronologically:
   - What was happening right before the issue surfaced?
   - What was the first sign something was wrong or suboptimal?
   - What did the user do next, step by step?
   - How did it resolve (or not)?
   - What happened immediately after?

3. **Identify the actors involved.** Who was present? What roles did they play? Were there bystanders, decision-makers, affected parties? Get names or role labels.

4. **Capture the environment and tools.** What software, hardware, physical space, or process was in use? What was the context (office, remote, mobile, urgent, routine)?

5. **Note emotional language.** Pay attention to frustration markers ("had to," "forced to," "ridiculous"), relief markers ("finally," "at least"), and avoidance markers ("I just don't bother anymore"). These signal friction points for Phase 2.

6. **Extract the specific outcome.** What was the tangible result of this case? Was it a lost sale, a delayed shipment, a wasted hour, a frustrated client? Quantify where possible.

7. **Validate concreteness.** If the user drifts into generalizations ("this happens all the time"), gently redirect: "Let me understand — in that specific instance on Tuesday, what exactly happened?" Do not proceed until at least one concrete case is fully documented.

## Suggested prompts

- "Walk me through the last time this was a real problem. Not in general — the actual most recent time."
- "What were you doing right before you noticed the issue?"
- "Take me through it minute by minute — what happened first, then what?"
- "Who else was involved in that specific situation? What did they do?"
- "What was the actual result — the concrete thing that happened because of this?"

## Exit condition
At least one fully reconstructed concrete case with timeline, actors, tools, emotional markers, and specific outcome. No unresolved abstractions remain.

## Output
- One or more concrete case narratives (chronological reconstruction)
- Timeline of events for each case
- List of actors and their roles per case
- Environment and tooling context
- Emotional marker catalog (frustration, relief, avoidance signals)
- Specific outcomes with any available quantification
